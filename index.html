Dennis Ritchie

September 9, 1941 - October 12, 2011

My work was fairly theoretical. It was in recursive function theory. And in particular, hierarchies of functions in terms of computational complexity. I got involved in real computers and programming mainly by being - well, I was interested even as I came to graduate school.

Timeline:

September 9, 1941

Dennis Ritchie was born on September 9th, 1941 in Bronxville New York

1963-1968

Ritchie earned his bachelor's degree in Physics in 1963, and completed his Doctorate degree in Mathematics in 1968 from Harvard University.

January 1, 1967

Ritchie started working at Bell Labs in 1967 where he worked on Multics operating system.

November 3, 1971

Unix was released on November 3rd, 1971, opening a operating system to work on a variety of platforms with highly adapted quality.

January 1, 1972

Ritchie and Thompson's operating system was not functioning on the more advanced PDP-11 mini computer in 1971. This prompted Ritchie to create C programming lanuage. C programming lanuage is very popular and important to the development of software including programs such as C++ and Java.

January 1, 1973

Ritchie and Thompson wrote UNIX using C programming language. This allowed for UNIX to work a wide variety of computers in a range of large and minicomputers and advanced to simple computers.

January 1, 1983

Ritchie and Thompson received the Turing Award "for their development of generic operating systems theory and specifically for the implementation of the UNIX operating system".

January 1, 1988

Ritchie was elected into the U.S National Academy of Engineering in 1988.

January 1, 1999

Ritchie was awarded the U.S. National Medal of Technology in 1999.

January 1, 1995

In 1995 Ritchie and his team released Plan 9 operating system. Plan 9 is an operating system that is originating at Bell Labs

April 1, 1996

Inferno is a distributed operating system that is released in 1966 by Ritchie and his team. Inferno was based on the experience gained with Plan 9 from Bell Labs.

January 26, 2011

Dennis Ritchie and Ken Thompson received the the Japan Prize for Information and Communications.

October 12, 2011

Ritchie died in October 12, 2011, Berkeley Heights, New Jersey, United States. He died after a long fight against prostate cancer and heart disease.
